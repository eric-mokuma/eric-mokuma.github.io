Neuroplasticity refers to the ability of neurons to change and remodel during their lives.

Research suggests that resilience is linked to cerebral plasticity, or neuroplasticity, which refers to the brain's ability to reorganise and restructure in response to environmental changes. Individuals' brain plasticity can be stimulated by consistent training and the repetition of new behaviours. Neuroscience helps us understand the underlying mechanisms of resilience by investigating how the brain handles and regulates stress and emotions.
